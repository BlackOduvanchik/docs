## Overview

Video spoofing lets you replace your real webcam feed with a virtual video source in browser profiles. This is useful for identity verification, video calls, and platforms that check webcam availability.

## How it works

When video spoofing is enabled, Oxygen Atmos intercepts the browser's media device enumeration and replaces it with a virtual video device. Websites see a webcam device but receive the spoofed feed instead of your real camera.

## Enable video spoofing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open profile editor">
    Click **Edit** on a profile in the dashboard.

    {/* Screenshot: profile edit button */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Advanced settings">
    Navigate to the **Advanced** tab in the profile editor.

    {/* Screenshot: advanced settings tab */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Video Spoofing">
    Toggle **Video Spoofing** to on.

    {/* Screenshot: video spoofing toggle */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure source">
    Choose the video source:

    | Source | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Image** | Display a static image as webcam feed |
    | **Video file** | Play a video file as webcam feed |
    | **Virtual camera** | Use a virtual camera app (OBS, ManyCam) |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The spoofed webcam will be active next time you launch the profile.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Video spoofing affects the `navigator.mediaDevices.enumerateDevices()` API response. Platforms that check for real hardware may detect virtual devices depending on the spoofing method used.
</Note>

## Use cases

- **Identity verification** — complete KYC checks that require a webcam
- **Video conferencing** — join calls with a spoofed feed
- **Platform checks** — pass device enumeration checks on antibot systems
